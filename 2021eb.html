2021 Moral Philosophy Workshop and Shanghai Interscholastic Ethics Bowl

Ethics Bowl China, Shanghai Interscholastic Ethics Bowl

This year, our Ethics Bowl program in Shanghai, China features an online Moral Philosophy Workshop from 1/31 to 2/3 and a face-to-face Ethics Bowl match on 2/7. The Moral Philosophy Workshop is an intense program designed to immerse high school students (Grade 9-12) in important issues in professional ethics and philosophy through seminar discussions, readings, and writings. We invited six speakers for the online seminars, including several university professors from Ethics Bowl communities across the globe. We also developed an online platform known as the Ethics Bowl Symposium for our workshop. Students will complete assignments, readings, and other collaborative activities on the Symposium. See below for our syllabus.


The 2/7 Ethics Bowl match is a face-to-face event. An Ethics Bowl match is like a debate, but it's far more than a debate: In the Ethics Bowl, teams are not required to pick opposing sides, nor is the goal to “win” the argument by knocking down the other team or its position. The Ethics bowl is, at heart, a collaborative discussion where teams are rewarded for the degree to which they eschew adversarial positioning and instead adopt a more collegial, collaborative stance. Both events are completely free (except for match-day meals). Click here to fill out the pre-registration form. You will be asked to set up an account after you have completed the form.
